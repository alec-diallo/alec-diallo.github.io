About Me

I am a final-year PhD student and an RA at the University of Edinburgh’s Institute for Computing Systems Architecture. My PhD research, funded by Arm, explores foundational aspects of Supervised & Unsupervised Learning for performance optimization, Adversarial Robustness of Machine Learning systems, and DNN Explainability. As member of the Mobile Intelligence Lab, my work with Dr Paul Patras primarily focuses on the design and optimisation of Artificial Intelligence systems for Cyber Security.

Broadly, my research interests include: AI/computational models of intelligence, optimization algorithms, cyber security, automated reasoning and fully-autonomous systems.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained a joint Computer Science and Electrical Engineering Degree from ESIEE Paris (École Supérieure d'Ingénieurs en Électrotechnique et Électronique) and Mundiapolis University.

Recent Publications

Sabre: Cutting through Adversarial Noise with Adaptive Spectral Filtering and Input Reconstruction

To appear in: IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy | 2024

Addressing the susceptibility of neural networks to input perturbations, we introduce a robust feature extraction methodology for building adversarially robust classifiers.

by Alec F. Diallo and Paul Patras

Deciphering Clusters with a Deterministic Measure of Clustering Tendency

Published in: IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering | 2023

This work proposes two new statistical measures for studying and evaluating clusters for unsupervised machine learning tasks.

by Alec F. Diallo and Paul Patras

Spider: Deep Learning-driven Sparse Mobile Traffic Measurement Collection and Reconstruction

Published in: IEEE Global Communications Conference | 2021

This paper introduces a Reinforcement Learning framework for cost-effective mobile traffic measurement collection and reconstruction.

by Yini Fang, Alec F. Diallo, Chaoyun Zhang, and Paul Patras

Adaptive Clustering-based Malicious Traffic Classification at the Network Edge

Published in: IEEE Conference on Computer Communications | 2021

Leveraging a modularity principle, we introduce the Adaptive Clustering architecture. A framework for generating optimal low-dimensional embeddings that maximize the classification accuracy of neural networks.

by Alec F. Diallo and Paul Patras
