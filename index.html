About Me

I am a final-year PhD student at the University of Edinburghâ€™s Institute for Computing Systems Architecture. Under the supervision of Dr Paul Patras, my research in the Mobile Intelligence Lab, supported by Arm, primarily focuses on designing and optimizing Artificial Intelligence systems for Cyber Security. Our prior work explored foundational aspects of Supervised & Unsupervised Learning for performance optimization, Adversarial Robustness of Machine Learning systems, and DNN Explainability. Recent Publications

Sabre: Cutting through Adversarial Noise with Adaptive Spectral Filtering and Input Reconstruction

Addressing the susceptibility of neural networks to input perturbations, we introduce a robust feature extraction methodology for building adversarially robust classifiers.

Alec F. Diallo and Paul Patras | 2024

Deciphering Clusters with a Deterministic Measure of Clustering Tendency

This work proposes two new statistical measures for studying and evaluating clusters for unsupervised machine learning tasks.

Alec F. Diallo and Paul Patras | 2023

Spider: Deep Learning-driven Sparse Mobile Traffic Measurement Collection and Reconstruction

This paper introduces a Reinforcement Learning framework for cost-effective mobile traffic measurement collection and reconstruction.

Adaptive Clustering-based Malicious Traffic Classification at the Network Edge
